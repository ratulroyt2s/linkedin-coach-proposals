The Real Problem

Where Investor Capital Is Slipping Through the Cracks

You've built one of the most credentialed profiles in real estate — Harvard, UCLA Health Board, a Biden administration role, and a Top 100 industry ranking. But investor relations at Forward One is still being managed the way it was done 20 years ago: manually, reactively, and at the mercy of an overcrowded inbox.

📬

Investor Inquiries Handled Ad Hoc

Interested investors submit inquiries and get a response when you have time — not within minutes. Every hour of delay reduces the probability of a scheduled call, and every missed inquiry is a potential LP lost to another fund.

🔄

No Automated LP Nurture Sequence

Prospects who express interest but don't commit immediately fall into a follow-up black hole. Without a systematic 30–60 day nurture sequence, "I need to think about it" quietly becomes "I invested elsewhere."

📡

Deal Flow Communications Are Scattered

Updates on acquisitions, asset management wins, and portfolio performance go out inconsistently — sometimes via email, sometimes on calls. There's no centralized communication cadence that keeps prospects warm and LPs informed.

📊

Quarterly Investor Updates Still Manual

Preparing and sending LP updates is time you spend that could be automated. Without a systematic quarterly communication workflow, updates are delayed, inconsistent in format, and leave LPs wondering about their investment.

🤝

No System to Convert Interested Investors to LPs

There's no structured pipeline that takes a warm prospect from first inquiry through qualification, investment committee call, and final commitment — it's stitched together manually for every single relationship.

🎓

Civic Authority Not Leveraged in a Content Funnel
